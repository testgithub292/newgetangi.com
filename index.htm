The Problem

As we mentioned earlier, major banks and card networks do not service the legal cannabis industry. While no specific federal law prohibits banks from servicing the cannabis market, the Federal Government oversees all banks and credit unions, resulting in most banks opting to remain on the sidelines until the legal framework extends beyond State-level regulations.

As a result, the industry faces four interconnected problems that have created a cash-only payment crisis for 8,000 retailers and 60 million consumers:

Lack of Banking Partners: The Key Barrier
The absence of banking partners is the single biggest hurdle preventing payment providers from cornering the untapped $33 Billion cannabis market. Without a banking partner, funds from cannabis transactions have no secure or legal destination, leaving payment providers in financial no man’s land and unable to operate within regulatory frameworks.

The Wild West of Non-Compliant Solutions
In the absence of a compliant option, makeshift providers have flooded the market, offering short-term risky and illegal payment alternatives. These solutions violate compliance rules, leading to inevitable shutdowns that force retailers back to cash-only operations

The Cash-Only Bottleneck
As a result, more than 8000 retailers face significant challenges from the limitations of cash-only payments. A typical dispensary handles up to $250,000 per week in cash, making it a target for armed robbery and needing armoured trucks to transport large volumes of money. Cash-only transactions also limit customer spending, complicate accounting, and hinder access to loans and other financial support

Collateral Damage
Meanwhile, 60 million consumers are forced to carry cash and are excluded from loyalty programs, which are standard in every other retail industry, as there is no way of tracking their spending habits. This is the harsh reality of cannabis payments—until now.
